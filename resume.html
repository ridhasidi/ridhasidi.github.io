Ridha Sidi Mulyawan

Earth Science and GIS Enthusiast

Ridha Sidi Mulyawan is an earth science enthusiast who is currently looking for new opportunities. He has broad experience ranging from geodata (geochemistry, geophysics, lithology, etc) analysis to knowledge dissemination. His background in earth science and his long-live passion in technology provide him the will to keep learning about GIS and its application.

His career started as a Research Assistant in a research center focusing on geothermal. His duties ranged from doing scientific geothermal research, designing pamphlets, leaflets, and books for workshops, facilitating workshops, courses, meetings among stakeholders, etc.

He went to France to continue his study in 2018, but eventually decided to drop out after he realized the program was not focused on geohazard and its mitigation efforts, but only on applied geophysics. After coming back from France, he worked as a site geologist to supervise tunnel boring for irrigation.

He always tries to gather some open-source geodata to help build a rapid assessment map each time natural hazard occurs. He believes using data during a hazard not only would help to streamline post-disaster relief efforts, but also to improve the pre-disaster mitigation efforts to reduce hazard risk in the future. After learning about Google Earth Engine (GEE), he recently built algorithms that could automatically read and analyze Sentinel-1 radar images to delineate the extent of the submerged area during flood.

His biggest dream is to be able to work by applying his knowledge on GIS and remote sensing to build a self-sufficient and resilient community. Please proceed to the Portfolio page to view some samples of his works.
